# KidCanvas Teacher Outreach Guide

## Quick Start: You Just Joined "Art Teacher Life Hacks" Facebook Group

Congrats! This is one of the BEST groups for reaching art teachers. Here's your action plan:

### Week 1: Observe & Engage (DO THIS FIRST)
**Don't post about KidCanvas yet.** The group will see you as a spammer.

Instead:
1. **Like and comment** on 10-15 posts about portfolio management, organizing artwork, or parent communication
2. **Share genuine advice** when teachers ask for help (don't mention KidCanvas)
3. **Build rapport** - be a helpful member first
4. **Watch for questions** like:
   - "How do you organize student artwork photos?"
   - "Best app for digital portfolios?"
   - "Parent-teacher conference portfolio tips?"

### Week 2: Soft Introduction
When you see a relevant question (portfolio management, artwork organization):

**Good Response Example:**
> "I was struggling with this too! I ended up building a simple tool to organize everything by student automatically. Happy to share if anyone wants to try it - it's free for teachers. DM me!"

**Don't:**
- Post the link publicly (seems spammy)
- Say "I built this app" without context
- Ignore the person's specific question

### Week 3: Value-First Post
Once you've been active for 2 weeks, create a value post:

**Example Post:**
> **How I Cut My Portfolio Prep Time from 3 Hours to 20 Minutes**
>
> Parent-teacher conferences used to be my nightmare. Sorting through hundreds of photos, printing samples, organizing by student...
>
> Here's what changed my workflow:
>
> 1. **Photograph in batches** - After each class, not at the end of week
> 2. **Tag immediately** - Name the file with student name while it's fresh
> 3. **Use a dedicated tool** - Not camera roll (I built KidCanvas for this, but anything works)
> 4. **Share digital-first** - Parents get links, only print for conferences
>
> Went from dreading conferences to having portfolios ready in minutes.
>
> Anyone else have workflow tips? What works for you?

### Ongoing Strategy: Be Helpful, Not Salesy
- Comment on 3-5 posts per day
- Answer questions genuinely
- Share KidCanvas only when directly relevant
- Offer to DM details instead of public posting
- Build relationships with active members

## Measuring Success

### Key Metrics to Track Weekly
- **Emails sent** vs **open rate** (25-40% is good)
- **Emails sent** vs **reply rate** (5-10% is good)
- **Signups from each channel** (email, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- **Engagement rate** on social posts
- **Best performing email template**
- **Best performing subject line**
- **Time invested** vs **signups gained**

### Red Flags to Watch
- Open rate below 15% (your subject lines are weak)
- Reply rate below 2% (your message isn't resonating)
- Zero signups after 50 contacts (landing page or offer issue)
- Getting marked as spam (slow down, improve targeting)

### Success Indicators
- Teachers reply with questions (they're interested)
- Teachers cc colleagues (word-of-mouth starting)
- Teachers share in Facebook groups organically
- Teachers request features (they're using it)
- Teachers tag you in Instagram posts (social proof building)

## FAQ: Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "I'm not getting any replies to my emails"
- Try different subject lines (test 3 variations)
- Send at different times (Tuesday 7am vs Thursday 4pm)
- Shorten your email (try 3 sentences max)
- Add personalization (mention their school or recent project)

### "Teachers seem interested but don't sign up"
- Friction in signup process? (test it yourself)
- Landing page unclear? (show to non-teacher, get feedback)
- Asking for too much info? (minimize required fields)
- Send direct signup link in follow-up

### "I'm getting marked as spam"
- Sending too many emails too fast (slow down)
- Using spam trigger words ("Free," "Click here," all caps)
- Buying email lists (never do this)
- Fix: Use professional email service (SendGrid, Mailchimp)

### "Facebook groups aren't engaging with my posts"
- Posting too much about KidCanvas (provide value first)
- Not active enough in general (comment more on others' posts)
- Wrong timing (post during teacher planning hours, not late night)
- Format: Ask questions, don't just share links
